Driving from Benin through Nigeria into Cameroon is less about distance and more about endurance.

The journey begins in Cotonou, where the route quickly transitions into the dense traffic corridors leading toward Lagos. Between the border and Nigeria’s commercial capital the road feels like a continuous settlement, with markets extending for long stretches and checkpoints appearing regularly along the route.

Further inland toward Abuja, roughly 50 checkpoints can appear between major cities. Despite the number of stops, most encounters remain manageable and are part of normal travel across the region.

East of Abuja the landscape changes again as the road climbs toward the Nigerian highlands near Lafia and Gembu. Distances become slower to cover, and accommodation options are limited in smaller towns.

From there, travelers heading toward Cameroon face several border crossing options. The Mbanga crossing is one of the lesser‑used routes and is normally chosen only by drivers prepared for extremely poor road conditions. Sections may include river crossings without bridges and heavily eroded tracks requiring slow off‑road driving.

Even short distances can take several hours. In extreme cases, 7 km may require more than 3 hours of driving depending on seasonal damage from rain.

This route represents one of the most demanding overland connections between Nigeria and Cameroon and is best approached with preparation, patience and flexible timing.
