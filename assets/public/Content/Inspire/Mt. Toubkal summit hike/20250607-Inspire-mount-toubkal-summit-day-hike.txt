Mount Toubkal rises to 4,167 m as the highest peak in North Africa, but the route to the summit feels more approachable than its altitude suggests.

Starting in the High Atlas Mountains, the trail climbs steadily through rocky valleys and small Berber settlements before entering a wide alpine landscape above the tree line. The higher you go, the more the terrain opens into long ridgelines and distant views across southern Morocco.

The full route covered about 26 km with roughly 2,300 m of ascent and descent and took around 10 hours in total. It is a long day, but technically straightforward in stable conditions.

Reaching the summit at sunrise reveals layer after layer of mountain ranges fading into the horizon. Despite its reputation as a serious altitude objective, Mount Toubkal is also one of the most scenic high summits that can be completed in a single push from the valley.
