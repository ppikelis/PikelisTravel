Kuata Island shark dive — Fiji

Fiji is not only palm trees and tropical beaches. Around Kuata Island it is also possible to dive with reef sharks in open water.

Before entering the water each diver is paired with an instructor carrying a metal rod used to gently redirect sharks if they approach too closely. Even knowing this, sitting behind a low rock barrier at about 12 m depth while several sharks circle nearby feels unreal.

The experience is closer to watching an aquarium from inside rather than from behind glass. Calm movements, controlled breathing and staying close to the group quickly become essential.

Despite the adrenaline, the dive remains surprisingly controlled and safe when guided properly.

Still, sharing open water with sharks at arm’s length is something that stays with you long after leaving the island.
