Kuata Island in Fiji is one of the few places in the Yasawa Islands where divers can experience guided reef shark encounters in relatively shallow water.

Most shark dives here take place at around 10–15 m depth behind natural rock formations that create a partial barrier between divers and the open approach zone used by the sharks. Professional guides remain close throughout the dive and may use metal rods to gently redirect animals if they swim too near the group.

The experience is designed for certified divers and is considered accessible compared with deeper offshore shark dives elsewhere in the Pacific. Visibility is typically high, which allows close observation of multiple reef shark species moving naturally through the area.

Because of the controlled setup and shallow depth, Kuata Island has become one of the most popular locations in Fiji for a first shark dive experience.
