Talofa from Samoa

Pristine beaches, waterfalls, turtles and very relaxed island life were exactly what brought us to Samoa.

Driving around the islands quickly revealed something else as well: color. Villages decorate fences, flower pots, houses and roadside areas with an enthusiasm that makes the entire country feel like a friendly competition between communities. Add thousands of roadside flags and Christmas lights — which remain year‑round in some places — and the result is a landscape that feels constantly festive.

Another surprise was the number of churches. Nearly every village seemed to have at least one, sometimes several, giving the impression that faith plays a central role in daily life across both Upolu and Savai‘i.

Beyond village life, Samoa delivers exactly what travelers imagine when they think about the South Pacific: coastal lava fields with crashing waves, rainforest waterfalls hidden deep inland, swimming holes surrounded by jungle and quiet beaches with almost no crowds.

Simple, welcoming and colorful — Samoa turned out to be one of the most relaxed island experiences in the Pacific.
