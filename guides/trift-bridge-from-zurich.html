My Experience

I did the Triftbrücke hike with a friend on a clear morning, starting straight from the parking lot at 9:30 – no gondola, just the trail and the climb ahead. The path gains height quickly, and within the first hour you already feel far away from the valley. It's steady work, but the kind that keeps you motivated because the landscape keeps opening up around you.

By 11:30 we reached the suspension bridge. Seeing it appear suddenly above the turquoise glacier lake is one of those moments that makes the whole ascent worth it. Standing there, with the wind moving the bridge slightly and the glacier right in front of us, felt wild and real in the best possible way.

We walked the entire route up and down – about 15 km with 1,200 m of ascent in roughly 5.5 hours. It's a proper hike, but very doable in a day and one of those routes I keep recommending to friends who want a big alpine experience without needing technical skills.
