Fiji

“Bula” and “Welcome home” is how people greet you in Fiji. It sounds like a phrase meant for visitors, but after a few days it starts to feel accurate.

We chose Fiji as a honeymoon destination and it turned out to be exactly the right decision. White sand beaches, palm trees, clear water and slow sunsets set the rhythm of the days almost immediately. Time moved differently there. Plans became optional.

What surprised me most was how much there was to see just below the surface. Reefs stretched for miles, and fish and small reef sharks often appeared only a few meters from the shore. It never required long boat trips or complicated logistics. You could step into the water and be surrounded by marine life within minutes.

Travelling between places like Nadi, the Yasawa Islands and Taveuni showed different sides of the country, but the atmosphere remained the same everywhere. Evenings often ended with kava ceremonies, singing and dancing that felt less like performances and more like part of everyday life.

Fiji was one of those rare destinations where doing very little felt like the right way to experience the country. And one of the few places I would return to for exactly the same reason again.
