Mount Cameroon

Linas and I arrived in Cameroon 1 day early during the rally because there was still one unfinished checkpoint from our previous trip: Mount Cameroon, the highest peak in the country at 4,085 m. Normally this hike takes 2 days. With nearly 3,000 m of ascent and about 25 km of walking, that makes sense. We decided to try it in 1.

The start was not ideal. Our flight was delayed, our luggage did not arrive, and traffic took longer than expected. In the end we slept about 2 hours before reaching the trailhead early in the morning. There is a yearly race on this mountain and the fastest runners finish in around 4–5 hours, so the plan still seemed realistic.

The path goes straight up from the beginning. There are almost no switchbacks. Some sections were steep enough that we had to climb on all fours. After a while our original guide dropped behind because he could not keep our pace. Later we met another guide on the mountain and continued together. He was wearing rubber boots.

We reached the summit after about 7.5 hours.

We expected the descent to be quick, but light rain made the volcanic rock slippery and slowed us down a lot. It took another 6 hours to get back down. The last hour was through jungle in the dark. We could hear animals moving somewhere around us, which made the final stretch longer than expected.

In total it became a 13-hour day on Mount Cameroon, with almost no sleep beforehand. It was not the original plan, but it turned out to be one of the more memorable climbs of the trip.
